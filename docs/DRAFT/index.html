◁ Dead Simple Blog Generator

DRAFT

In the current day and era I guess most of us have a ton of bookmarked content, and considerable amount of internal notes and documents and whatnot that we keep in services like Google Keep and Obsidian.

For a couple of years now I have been searching for a frictionless way to publish this content. Social networks come and go, so the most future proof medium always struck me as the good old blog.

My ideal workflow would be to write or edit the content the way I already do for my personal wiki, that current consists of markdown files in an obsidian vault, and issue a command to have everything transformed into a static website, with a RSS feed updated accordingly, that I could host is services like GitHub Pages, Netlify or Firebase.

With that in mind, I set out to investigate what was available. Behemoths like Wordpress and self hosted Ghost were out of the picture from the get go, as I wanted something lightweight and simple.

Hugo claims of speed were alluring, but I ended up finding it too complex. In Jekyll's case, I believe the problem was with liquid, their own templating engine, and the Ruby dependencies. I also looked into Grav, Eleventy, Pelican and Nikola, but was ultimately unsatisfied, for a reason or another. So I set out to build my own static site generator. My first attempt was in Rust, and I was actually making slow but steady progress with it in my free time.

Fast forward to around September 2024, when I go fed up with how much Python sucks for large codebases, and started to look for a static typed and ideally compiled language with whom I could be as productive as I am in Python. I decided to give Go a try and, after reading a few books and trying my hand in a minor project, I decided to port what I already had in Rust to Go. In a couple of days the Go version already had feature parity with the barebones Rust implementation. A short 3 months later, I am proud to present the Dead Simple Blog Generator (DSBG), a static site generator written in Go.
